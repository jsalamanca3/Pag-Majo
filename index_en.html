How does muscular power evolve in adolescent volleyball practitioners?

Introduction

Volleyball is a sport with high integrity in jumping power, which leads to several useful parameters such as its vertical distance, its muscular performance in the lower limbs and progression of aerobic efforts, so we decided to investigate how volleyball develops. potency in adolescents between 12 and 18 years. Next, you will find the documents that were used for the investigation and an interview with an international professional who talks to us about the subject.

Chronological and biological differentiation

This research is based on the development of power in young people from 12 to 18 years old, so it was necessary to inquire about the biological and chronological ages in order to understand how to carry out an effective training, Thus, in the article "Effect of plyometric training on the explosive strength of pubertal girls practicing volleyball" and "Analysis of the vertical jump of first category volleyball players" we found that plyometric training in volleyball gives importance to the degrees of maturation, for a good intramuscular coordination, the improvement of the synchronization of muscular activity and mitotic activity. This gives a performance in jumping, explosive strength, endurance and balance. The biological dimension, takes as an indicator the parameters related to the physical performance of volleyball players, specifically analyzing the jumping power, since it is a determinant game action for the sport, in addition to this each athlete should be trained under individualization to prioritize their physiological differences and generate necessary adaptations such as explosive strength, muscle power and emphasizing the muscle groups worked in the sport, presenting a greater recruitment of fibers with predominance in fast fibers (2A) and intramuscular coordination.

The Plyometry

It is well known that the plyometric method is that expression that activates the muscles by performing an eccentric-concentric contraction as fast as possible improving the responsiveness of the central nervous system and intramuscular coordination. Thus, it is one of the most used methods for the development of power as shown in the article "The eccentric-concentric phase and the index of reactive strength in the jump with counter movement in volleyball players" as it develops power, improves the vertical jumping ability and eccentric strength and at the same time the speed of concentric contractions, since it generates great muscular tensions, creating neuronal adaptations and likewise promotes the response of the nervous impulse from the spinal cord, the development of the synchronization and activation of the muscular fibers and the increase of the recruitment of type 2 fibers since it increases the threshold of rupture of the muscle and the capacity to absorb loads. On the other hand, this method can also improve aspects such as speed in changes of direction, muscle strength and collagen synthesis in the connective tissue allowing a better functioning of the tendon in such a way that injuries can be prevented.

Complementary methods

Continuing with the research process focused on improving the muscular power of volleyball players, alternative methods were found that complement the physiological adaptations from training, in the articles "Architectural and functional muscular adaptations after 12 weeks of stretching in adolescent athletes" and "Photobiomodulation therapy and NMES improve muscle strength and jumping performance in young volleyball athletes: a randomized controlled trial study in Brazil" suggest that pre- and post-training stretching facilitates muscle and joint interaction allowing the muscle groups involved to generate greater stress, which implies that the body must adapt to this type of training loads allowing the development of power with less risk of injury. On the other hand, we found that neuromuscular electrical stimulation plays an important role in groups of athletes who are at a stage of biological development close to the threshold, being applied before plyometric training promoting greater excitability of muscle contractions, thus improving the motor activity so that the muscles to be trained present an adaptation to the proposed power exercises, since the activation of these muscles causes the motor plate or neuromuscular junction has the ability to transmit nerve impulses more easily to the muscle fiber.
